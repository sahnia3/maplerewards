The short version: MapleRewards is in strong shape and close to launch. Everything a customer actually touches works, and — just as important — it now tells the truth: the numbers it shows are real. It's not deployed yet, and there's a short, ordinary list of "flip-the-switch" steps left, but the product itself is solid and worth charging for.

What MapleRewards is

A Canada-first credit-card rewards advisor. It knows the cards you carry, the points you hold, and how the Canadian rewards market works — and turns that into clear answers: which card to use for a purchase, what your points are actually worth, how to fly on points, what's about to expire, and which promotions are worth acting on. The big idea is that no one else does all of this for Canada, personalized to you — the comparison sites are generic, and the good blogs aren't personal.

What works right now

  • The optimizer — tells you the best card for any purchase, in real dollars, and is genuinely Canada-smart (e.g. it warns that Amex won't work at Costco or the Loblaws stores and points you to the right backup).
  • Your wallet & points — track every card and balance, edit them in one tap, and see the real CAD value instantly.
  • Trip planner — finds award flights across 12+ airline programs (not just Aeroplan/United), and is honest about which prices are live versus estimated.
  • Maple, the AI advisor — answers in plain English, knows your balances, and proactively warns about things like an upcoming Aeroplan devaluation before it costs you. Clean, readable answers.
  • Pro tools — shows what you left on the table, which card credits are about to expire, your status progress, and card "stacks" tailored to how you actually spend.
  • Promos & news — live transfer-bonus alerts and a constantly-refreshed Canadian rewards news feed.
  • Catalog, comparisons & calculators — browse every card, compare any two head-to-head, and check what a points balance is worth — all free, no sign-in.

The condition, honestly

The most valuable thing this pass did was make the product trustworthy. Almost everything that needed fixing was about the accuracy of numbers, not broken buttons — for example, one points program (Air Miles) was being valued about 70× too low, and a few pieces of data were listed twice. Those are now corrected and double-checked. That matters a lot for a paid product: people will stop trusting it the moment a number looks wrong.

Underneath, the engineering is healthy: the automated test suite passes, the app builds cleanly, and the security checks came back clean. Nothing a customer clicks is broken.

Before you flip the switch

Ordinary launch steps, in plain terms:

  • Code is committed and pushed. ✓ Done.
  • Apply the data fixes to the live database — they're proven on the test copy; they just need to run on production.
  • Run one more automated code review — the last cloud run crashed on their side before finishing; worth a clean pass.
  • Make a call on two small data questions (the duplicate transfer ratio, and pricing premium-card perks) — or knowingly ship as-is.
  • Double-check the live settings & keys are in place (payments, AI, flight data).
Bottom line: Yes — this is ready to be a paid product, and you're close to deploy. It works, it's honest, and it's genuinely useful in a way the competition isn't. Knock out the short checklist above and launch with confidence.
